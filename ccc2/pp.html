Youth Sprint Canoe-Kayaking

Training for the Sprint Racing Program takes place year round. This program is designed to be in line with Canoe/Kayak Canada's Long Term Athlete Development Framework and principles. We have divided our program will extend year round. Athletes are able to progress from the Youth Paddling Program to Development to our full time High Performance Sprint Program under the guidance of our coaching staff.

2014 Summer Learn to Paddle Program

Our Youth Learn to Paddle Program offers an all around paddling experience. The program is geared to a two-week session. We feel that participants have the opportunity to experience all facets of paddling and become comfortable in the racing boats over this period of time. On water activities include paddling in recreation canoes, whitewater kayaks, sprint racing canoes and kayaks, the 14 person sprint war canoe, and 20 person dragon boat.

Basic water safety is covered along with all the basics of the equipment and strokes and skills for the various boats.

Dry land activities will make up the remainder of the day. The emphasis is on active games and events in and around the Clubhouse (soccer, capture the flag, the CCC amazing race, etc).

We realize a one week program may be more suitable for some looking for a brief introduction to paddling. Therefore, participants may sign up for a one week session. We will offer limited spaces for one week camps. One week sessions are only available for the first week of all two week sessions.

To register, choose which session you would like to start and for how many consecutive sessions you would like to participate in.

All programs take place at the Calgary Canoe Club site. The Clubhouse offers an indoor paddling tank, hall area, and sprint bay specifically for camp participants. In poor weather situations we may move activities indoors.

Pre and post care available from 8:30am - 5:30pm under the supervision of our boat bay staff, must note on your registration form.

Outrigger Canoeing

Course & Program Description

In 2014 we plan to pick up where we left off with our program development. We have purchased a couple of new outrigger canoes (an OC1 and OC2) and with all of the new recruits in 2013 we intend to get to more races. Club fun regatta's proved to be very popular so we will add a few of those to our schedule along with a number of away regatta's.


Structure of Fees

  • First year outrigger program: General membership fee + $100
  • Regular Outrigger program: General membership fee + $175
  • In addition, an annual fee of $25 paid to CORA, the National governing body, is required for any paddler attending a CORA race.

We encourage new members to participate at the Recreation OC6 sessions on Wednesday evenings and Saturday morning to learn the basic skills in the 6 man boat. Once you are comfortable in the OC6 you may move onto the 1 and 2 person outrigger canoes. An orientation session from Lynda or a volunteer coach is required. Once you sign up we will email regular updates on training sessions and all program activities to keep you updated.

Intro to Outrigger:

Intro to Outrigger is mandatory for all new paddlers wanting to join the outrigger program at the Club, this course offers an introduction to outrigger paddling basics. Outrigger paddling is a competitive based program. Novices are welcome to join and try it out as a new sport. Paddle for fun, fitness or competition.

Drop-in sessions are every Wednesday 6:30pm – 8:00 pm, May 14 - October 1st. Weather permitting.

All guests must check in at the boat bay office and fill in a waiver form.
